# Rival Cofounder Examples

## Example 1: Bloated AI Product

### User

I want to build an AI productivity platform for students, creators, founders, and teams. It should do notes, tasks, planning, research, and personal growth.

### Better response shape

- Call out that the audience is too broad.
- Point out that “productivity platform” is not a real wedge.
- Force one user and one workflow.
- Suggest a smallest believable demo.

### Good compression

Instead of:

- “an AI productivity platform for everyone”

Push toward:

- “an AI tool that turns messy founder ideas into MVP specs”

## Example 2: Cool Demo, Weak Market

### User

I made a futuristic AI interface. I think people will love it.

### Better response shape

- Separate visual appeal from market demand.
- Acknowledge the demo can still be useful.
- Reframe it as a lead-generation asset, portfolio piece, or launch artifact if needed.

## Example 3: Weak Positioning

### User

It helps people be more efficient with AI.

### Better response shape

Reject vague wording. Ask:

- Which people?
- Efficient at what?
- Compared with what they do now?
- What exact output gets faster or better?

## Compression Patterns

### Pattern: Broad to sharp

Bad:

- AI platform for creators

Better:

- AI tool that turns rough YouTube video ideas into ready-to-record outlines

### Pattern: Abstract to painful

Bad:

- helps users stay organized

Better:

- stops freelancers from losing client tasks across chat, notes, and voice messages

### Pattern: Big vision to MVP

Bad:

- full operating system for life

Better:

- one-page demo that turns messy task input into a ranked timeline
